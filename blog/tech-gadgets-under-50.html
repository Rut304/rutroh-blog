3 Cheap Tech Buys I Actually Use Every Day

March 06, 2026

I've bought probably 30+ gadgets in the last year trying to optimize my home office setup. Most of them are collecting dust in a drawer. But these three? I genuinely use them daily, and none of them cost more than fifty bucks. No hype, just stuff that works.

Anker PowerCore 10000 — $21.99

I bought this because I kept killing my phone battery running Ollama's web interface while walking around the house (yes, I check inference times while making coffee, don't judge me). It's about the size of a credit card but thicker, charges my iPhone 15 roughly 2.5 times on a full charge, and I've had it since January without any issues.

The thing I didn't expect to love: it charges fast. Like, noticeably faster than some wall chargers I've used. Something about their PowerIQ detection — it figures out your device and maxes out the charge speed. I don't know the technical details honestly, it just works.

One complaint: the LED battery indicator is four tiny dots that are hard to see in daylight. Minor, but annoying.

Echo Dot (5th Gen) — $49.99

Look, I resisted Alexa for years. Privacy concerns, didn't want Amazon listening, the whole thing. Then I set up my AI automation stack and realized... I already have microphones everywhere (webcam, phone, laptop). So I gave in.

Turns out it's actually useful for hands-free stuff while I'm coding. "Alexa, set a timer for 25 minutes" for Pomodoro sessions. "Alexa, what's the weather" before I decide if I'm working from the porch. Quick math calculations when I'm too lazy to open a calculator.

The speaker quality surprised me — it's not great for music (don't expect bass), but for podcasts and voice responses it's perfectly fine. I paid $27 on a sale, which made it a no-brainer. At full price it's still worth it if you have any smart home stuff.

Wyze Cam v3 — $35.98

I bought this mostly because I work from home and wanted to keep an eye on the front door without getting up every time FedEx shows up with another SSD. (Running local AI models eats storage like crazy.)

The color night vision is legitimately impressive at this price. I can see my driveway clearly at 2 AM, which is better than security cameras I've seen at three times the cost. It's weather-rated too — mine's been sitting outside through a Massachusetts winter with zero issues.

Free cloud storage for motion clips, or you can throw in a microSD card for continuous recording. I use the SD card because... well, I don't love cloud storage for security footage. Call me paranoid.

The app is a little clunky — takes about 3-4 seconds to load the live feed, and occasionally it drops the connection for a few seconds. Not deal-breaking, but if you need split-second reliability for actual security, spend more money on a Ring or Ubiquiti setup.
